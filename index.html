Abstract

Recently, there has been a growing demand for conversational speech synthesis (CSS) that generates more natural speech by considering the conversational context. However, existing models often struggle with selecting emotionally appropriate prompts and expressing dynamic emotional changes. To address this, we introduce [XXX], a novel CSS framework that achieves superior emotional expressiveness and dynamism. We propose a retrieval module based on Valence-Arousal-Dominance (VAD) to select emotionally salient reference audio, and an intra-utterance modulation mechanism to generate lifelike emotional contours. To mitigate the issue of emotional data scarcity, we employ a GRL-based adversarial training strategy to disentangle speaker and emotion, effectively creating a diverse emotional corpus for retrieval. Our experimental results demonstrate that [XXX] excels in synthesizing speech that is not only contextually appropriate but also rich in emotional dynamics, significantly improving conversational naturalness.
